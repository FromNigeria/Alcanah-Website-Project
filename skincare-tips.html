Know your Skin Type

It is important to know your skin type in order to make an excellent decision about a proper skin care

routine and solutions on how to get a well balance skin.
If you do not use the suitable treatments, it can cause unwanted side effects and worsen your skin condition. The skin type is determined when you are born, However, when you grow up, it depends on your diet and way you take care of yourself.
Normal skin: Wipe the tissue on your face. If it does not reveal any oil, it means that you have normal skin. Because normal skin type is the least problematic, skin is always vibrant, supple, and elastic.
Dry skin: The tissue does not show any oil. Moreover, your face skin may feel dry, flaky, and tight when you wipe your face skin with a tissue.
Oily skin: The tissue reveals oil when you wipe the areas such as the nose, cheeks, and forehead. Oily skin is shiny, thick, and greasy. Oily skin is the problematic skin type.It has enlarged skin pores and acne breakouts. Unlike dry skin, people with oily skin do not have wrinkles and early aging signs.
Sensitive skin: If you have a sensitive skin type, your skin may feel dry and tight when you wipe it with tissue.Moreover, sensitive skin type may develop scaly and reddish areas.
Note: Cleansing your face skin should be repeated daily in the morning and at night. Make sure that you cleanse your skin gently and properly to remove makeup residue and dirt from your skin.

Stretch Marks Remedy

Stretch marks are part of our life. A stretch mark is deeper, like a scar and so can be caused by

a stretching of the skin.
Risk Factors - Anyone can develop stretch marks, but there are some factors that increase your likelihood of getting them, including:
  • Having a personal or family history of stretch marks
  • Being pregnant, especially for younger women
  • Being overweight
  • Gaining or loosing weight rapidly
The elastic fibres in your skin can tear and make long thin scars called stretch marks. Mostly women tend to get more than men. But men too are prone to it… especially those gym going guys. It can occur on stomach, thighs, upper arms, lower back, breast or an any part of the body. They fade with time, sort of turn silvery- white in appearance and really are not so noticeable.
Some tips to help prevent/ fade them faster.
  • Keep yourself hydrated - Drink 8–10 glasses of water everyday
  • Moisturize your skin well - Olive oil, Coconut oil, Cocoa butter, shea butter are all wonderful.
  • maintain a healthy weight.
  • Use of Natural African soap like Zee Black soap helps to minimize the scar left while bathing daily due to the healing power of Native Honey and Aloe Vera
  • If you are pregnant - go for slow and gradual weight gain
  • See a Doctor - if you noticed the stretch marks covers large areas of your body. Your Doctor can help determine the cause of your stretch marks and discuss viable treatments options with you.
